## What Is Acupuncture?

Acupuncture and Chinese herbal medicine have been used for thousands of years as a safe, effective approach for a wide range of conditions — including allergies, chronic pain, arthritis, lower back pain, migraines and headaches, women's reproductive health, orthopedic and sports injuries, and concerns such as anxiety, addiction, and phobias.

## Why Patients Often Choose Acupuncture

For many people, the first line of relief for chronic pain is nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory medication, or, for severe pain, prescription medication. These approaches are not always enough, and the side effects can become limiting over time. Acupuncture offers a different path — one that some patients pursue alongside conventional care, others as a primary option.

## Conditions Acupuncture Is Often Used For

- Fibromyalgia
- Carpal tunnel syndrome
- Lower back pain
- Menstrual cramps
- Myofascial pain — pain in trigger-point areas of the muscles
- Neck pain
- Headaches and migraines
- Osteoarthritis and knee pain
- Tennis elbow
- Postoperative dental pain

Ongoing research continues to evaluate where acupuncture is most effective. Evidence for osteoarthritis, neck pain, and knee pain is well established; results for myofascial pain and tennis elbow have been mixed.

## What to Expect During Your Session

A short conversation at the start to understand what you are working with that day. Then a quiet 30–45 minutes of treatment on the table. Most patients describe the experience as deeply restful.

## How It Pairs With Our Programs

Frequently used as part of an integrative approach for cancer care, hormonal balance, autoimmune support, and pain management.
