What to Expect at your First Visit

Our equipment is the FDA  approved Colenz device. It is an open  system and utilizes a very small speculum-- about the size of a small straw-- to inject water that has been filtered three times into your colon. With a gentle pressure, this device allows for a deep cleanse. The treatment is not painful but for some it might be slightly uncomfortable.

​

The device is safe and easy to use and does not require a therapist to be in the room with you during the entire procedure. As the recipient of the treatment, you will be lying comfortably on your back on the treatment table.  Once the speculum is inserted, and the water turned on,  your therapist will evaluate you  to make sure your colonic goes smoothly.  Your therapist will then leave the room to allow you to relax , listen to music or meditate

## What Should I Eat Before/After the Colonic?

Our recommendation is to do what is most comfortable for you.  Many people find that eating only lightly before or after the colonic, or even fasting the day of the treatment helps them feel that the cleanse has been more complete. Perhaps the best way to cleanse the digestive tract is to fast and to take 1-2 colonics during the fast.

How Often Should I have a Colonic?

﻿Our doctors will evaluate you to determine your course of treatment. Generally, we recommend a series of four to six treatments, one week apart.  Like many therapies, one treatment will bring some relief, but in order to have a lasting improvement, generally more than one colonic is needed.  After having a series of treatments, you will know what it feels like to detoxify.

If you have any questions, feel free to ask before the treatment.
